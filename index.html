About

What I work on

Malware Detection

Learning-based malware classification using static and structure-aware representations, including bytes/opcodes and control-flow graphs (CFGs).

Explainable AI (XAI)

Explanations for model decisions (feature attributions, subgraph explanations, and region-based methods) to support analyst trust and debugging.

Research Focus

Deep learning, interpretability, and robustness

I’m interested in bridging practical malware pipelines with rigorous ML evaluation. This includes:

  • Representations: bytes, opcodes, CFGs, and hybrid combinations.
  • XAI: attribution and explanation methods that highlight actionable evidence for analysts.
  • Adversarial robustness: understanding how packing/obfuscation and problem-space perturbations affect detection.
  • Reproducible evaluation: careful dataset curation, splits, and stress tests.
